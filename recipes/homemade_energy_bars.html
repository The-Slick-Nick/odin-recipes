Homemade Energy Bars

Description

Do you love Clif bars but hate the price & added sugar? I do, which is why I though up this recipe for my own version - using only cheap, whole ingredients and only fresh fruit as a source of sugar!

Ingredients

  • 3 Bananas, medium to large
  • 50 grams of peanut butter (about 3 tablespoons)
  • 150 grams of quick oats or rolled oats (about 1 1/2 cups)
  • 1 tablespoon of cinnamon

Equipment

Required

  • Food dehydrator or oven
  • Large bowl
  • Fork
  • Spoon

Recommended/Optional

  • Food Scale

Steps

  1. Peel the bananas and place them into a bowl
  2. Using a fork (or alternative) mash the bananas into a paste
  3. Add 50 grams of peanut butter to the banana mash and mix in
  4. Add 1 tablespoon of cinnamon to the mixture and continue mixing
  5. Add 150 grams of oats to the mixture and mix until the oats have the banana & peanut butter mixture evenly applied throughout.
  6. [Optional] Add additional food items to the mixture & mix in if desired. Some ideas include:
    • Raisins
    • Chopped peanuts (or other nut)
    • Granola
    • Chocolate Chips
    • Dates
  7. On your food dehdrator trays, spoon the mixture out into evenly-sized bar shapes. I recomment 15 total (5 per banana), but this can be adjusted to preference.
  8. Turn on food dehydrator and allow roughly 8 hours to dehyrdate fully (I usually leavte it overnight)
  9. Alternatively, if using an oven, spoon evenly-sized bar shapes onto a baking sheet lined with parchment paper and bake for about 20 minutes at 300 degrees Fahrenheit.
  10. Once complete, remove from dehydrator trays (or baking sheet) into an airtight container and optionally refrigerate. Enjoy as desired!
