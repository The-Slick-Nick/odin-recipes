Scrambled Eggs

Description

This is my own personal recipe for scrambled eggs. It is not fancy - it is basic & simple, but it is easy to make, cheap, nutritious, and quite tasty.

Ingredients

  • 5 large to medium-sized eggs
  • 150 grams of broccoli, frozen or fresh (about 1 stalk>
  • 80 grams of onion (1 small onion or 1/2 of one large onion)
  • 1 small to medium sized jalapeño pepper
  • 1 small handful of cilantro
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 teaspoon black pepper
  • 1 teaspoon of extra virgin olive oil (5g)

Equipment

Required

  • Stove or hot-plate
  • 1 medium to large sized skillet, saute pan, or frying pan
  • 2 medium sized bowls (large enough to contain 5 raw eggs)
  • 1 spatula
  • 1 fork
  • 1 cutting board
  • 1 knife, appropriate for chopping vegetables

Optional/Recommended

  • Food scale
  • Oil Sprayer
  • Measuring spoons

Steps

  1. Begin by chopping the broccoli into medium-sized (~1 inch width/diameter) chunks, placing them into a medium-sized bowl for temporary storage.
  2. Begin pre-heating your frying pan - place your frying pan onto your stove burner or hot plate with heat turned to medium.
  3. Crack 5 eggs into a medium-sized bowl.
  4. Add 1/2 teaspoon of salt and 1/2 teaspoon of black pepper to the cracked eggs. Stir the mixture toegether using a fork (or alternative stirring device).
  5. By this point, the pan should be warm enough to begin sautéing your broccoli. Apply your olive oil to the now-warmed pan and add the broccoli into the pan. During the next several steps, stir/sautee the broccoli around as needed using a spatula.
  6. Dice your onion (~80g, 1 small or 1/2 of a large) and add to the egg mixture.
  7. Dice your jalapeño pepper and add to the egg mixture.
  8. Dice your cilantro and add to the egg mixture.
  9. Stir egg mixture with a fork.
  10. Now, add the egg mixture into the frying pan. Using a spatula, mix and stir the egg mixture with the sauteeing broccoli.
  11. Over the next 5 to 10 minutes, occassionally stir the egg mixture in the pan around with a spatula, keeping raw, liquid egg in contact with pan surface as much as possible. As the egg cooks and a surface develops, stir and mix the egg around to bring in new, raw egg to the hot surface.
  12. As the entire mixture becomes solid & cooked, turn the heat on your burner or hot down to medium-low and continue to occassionally stir for another few minutes until completely cooked through.
  13. Turn the heat off on your burner or hot plate and let the eggs rest for another 5 minutes. If your frying pan (or alternative cooking surface) has a lid, keep the lid on for this period.
  14. Serve and enjoy!
